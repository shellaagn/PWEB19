For travellers, Medan is famous as the entry point to North Sumatra.
The influence of the Dutch and all other cultures that have made their
mark in the city are clearly imprinted on the metropolis, as shown by
the many attractions and architectural landmarks found everywhere in Medan.

Walk through the historical places in Medan, from a Chinese Socialite heritage Tjong A Fie Mansion
to an architectural wonder that implemented Malay, Italian, Spain, and Indian touch Istana Maimum;
the archaic area of Kesawan District that still has some resemblance of how Medan looked like decades
ago, to the hustling bustling Little India at K.H Zainul Arifin Street. If you loves nature and wildlifes,
Rahmat Gallery boasts a collection of thousands species from all over the world, and Penangkaran Buaya
Asam Kumbang is home to hundreds of crocodiles! There's also Graha Maria Annai Velangkanni, which is a
church with a long history adorned with intricate and beautiful carvings all over the building.
